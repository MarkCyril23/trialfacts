Background

Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and the Importance of Exercise

Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) causes a slight but noticeable decline in mental abilities, such as memory loss and impaired thinking. People with MCI have a much higher risk of going on to develop dementia.

The Power of Regular Exercise

Previous research has shown that regular exercise may help improve brain health and prevent dementia. This study is dedicated to comparing the effectiveness of three different kinds of exercise on memory and thinking abilities.

Our Research Aim

At the Research Centre at The University of Sydney, we are committed to advancing our understanding of cognitive health and finding practical solutions to improve the lives of individuals dealing with cognitive decline. Led by Dr. Maria Fiatarone Singh, our team is at the forefront of groundbreaking research in the field of memory improvement through exercise.
